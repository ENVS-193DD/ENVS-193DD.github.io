b. Make a new figure

Use a combination of ggplot(), geom_() and facet_wrap() (along with any associated functions to make your plot look nicer) to create a plot with total count on the x-axis and species on the y-axis with separate panels for each eBird group.

For full credit, be sure to:

  • label the x- and y-axes
  • get rid of the space between the bottom of the column and the axis line
  • use custom colors
  • get rid of the legend (which is a redundant feature)

Annotate all your code to demonstrate your understanding of what you have written.

Weird looking y-axis?

If your y-axis looks off, pay attention to what species are included in each group. Does it make sense that there is an axis tick for “Mallard” under “Shorebirds”? (Hint: no.)

  • scales = "free_x"
  • scales = "free_y"
  • scales = "free"

to see how the axes might change. Choose the option that makes most sense for your figure.
